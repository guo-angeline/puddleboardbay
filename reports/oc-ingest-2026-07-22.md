## Coordinate corroboration was the best of any batch

Newport is well mapped in OSM, so for the first time most candidates could be checked against an independently mapped beach rather than only against imagery.

**This refines the standing rule.** The statewide inventory said CCC coordinates are always locators and must always be re-derived. In Newport that was too pessimistic: 4 of 7 CCC pins land within 41 m of an independently mapped beach and are fine as stored. The honest version is **corroborate every pin, and re-derive only the ones that fail**, rather than assuming all are wrong. Blanket distrust costs real work and, worse, invites replacing a good coordinate with a hand-estimated one.

## Tides: the trap this batch nearly walked into

Orange County had **no tide station of its own**. It did not fail loudly, because the nearest listed station was Los Patos at 9 to 12 miles, comfortably inside `MAX_STATION_MI`. Every Newport spot would have rendered a confident tide reading taken from **a different harbor**, and the new coverage test would have passed.

Added Newport Bay Entrance (9410580) and Balboa Pier (9410583), both confirmed against the datagetter before being added. Newport spots now resolve from 0.1 to 2.6 miles.

**The lesson upgrades the one from the San Diego batch.** That batch taught: a gating boolean needs a test that it can be honoured. This one adds: **the test's threshold is a floor, not a definition of correct.** "Within range" and "relevant" are different claims, and only the first is mechanically checkable. Worth remembering wherever a distance cut stands in for a quality judgment.
